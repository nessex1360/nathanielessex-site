I am the artist in a family of Caribbean Catholic military engineers. Bridges and chapels on one side of the dinner table — me on the other, drawing.

Twelve years in corporate finance later, the engineering DNA is still there. It just runs underneath everything I build. MBA, fintech strategist, fluent in pricing models and credit controls. I've helped startups grow into full enterprises, built businesses from scratch, and run pricing inside one of the world's largest logistics companies. Wherever the spreadsheet meets the story, that's the work I do.

The actual trick isn't expertise in any one domain. It's far transfer — taking a pattern from your portfolio and applying it to your team, your kid, your novel, your launch. Most people stop at the first domain. I keep going.

Off the clock I'm an orchestral musician, a husband, and a father. I write fiction in the early mornings — long books with maps in the front. (Yes, that kind of guy.)

I'm still the artist at that table. The table is bigger now, and the only mission worth keeping is service. The work is the work; the point of the work is to be a useful citizen.

— Nathan
