CargoSprint · Strategy / Analytics

Cross-Platform Green Space & Market Share Analysis

3 Days

From brief to $250K–$1M opportunity sized and delivered

Project image — placeholder

Organization

CargoSprint / EModal

Role

Senior Data Analyst

Function

Sales, Strategy

Timeline

2022 – Present

Context

CargoSprint acquired EModal, combining two freight payment platforms — SprintPay and EModal — under one organization. Leadership needed a cross-platform view of the combined customer base to understand where the real revenue opportunity was: who wasn't a customer yet, who could be upgraded, and where conversion from one side of a transaction to the other was possible.

No unified customer master existed across the two systems. Payor and payee relationships — the same company appearing on both sides of a freight transaction depending on context — further complicated the picture. Entity resolution had to come first.

Approach

  • Merged customer data from SprintPay and EModal against three external freight market registries: NVOCC, FMC, and IATA
  • Built a Python normalization script to unify payor and payee data into a single canonical company master — resolving the same entity appearing as both sides of a transaction across data sources
  • Classified revenue opportunities into four types: upsell (existing customers spending more), cross-sell (customers on one platform not yet on the other), payor-to-payee conversion, and net-new (companies in the registries with no current relationship)
  • Segmented the analysis across air and ocean freight verticals to match sales motion by industry
  • Sized the Southeast region's opportunity at 10% capture as the primary scenario presented to leadership

Outcome

$250K – $1M

Estimated net new annual revenue — Southeast region at 10% upsell capture

Delivered in three days. Provided sales and revenue leadership with a prioritized view of upsell, cross-sell, conversion, and net-new targets across air and ocean freight — segmented and actionable without additional manual interpretation.

The canonical company master produced as a side output became a reusable foundation for future cross-platform analysis.
